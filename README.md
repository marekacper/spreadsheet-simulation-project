# Pricing Strategy Optimization for Urban Transport Providers

## Academic Context
This project was developed as part of a university curriculum for the "Simulations in Spreadsheets" course. It represents a comprehensive application of quantitative methods to solve real world business problems within a controlled academic environment.

## Project Objective
The primary goal of this study is to analyze and optimize the pricing policy for an urban transport provider, using MPK Kielce as a primary case study. The research focuses on finding a sustainable balance between public service accessibility and corporate profitability amidst fluctuating operational costs and financial forecasts for the 2023 2024 period(final_report.pdf) (p. 8).

## Advanced Spreadsheet Simulations
The entire analytical engine of this project was built within Microsoft Excel (SWAK final.xlsm) utilizing its advanced simulation capabilities. The model serves as a robust decision support system, processing extensive data on revenue streams and cost structures(final_report.pdf) (p. 7), (final_report.pdf) (p. 9), (final_report.pdf) (p. 13). By leveraging VBA macros and built in statistical tools, the simulation accounts for complex variables such as fuel consumption across different fleet types and city budget subsidy levels(final_report.pdf) (p. 1), (final_report.pdf) (p. 7), [10](final_report.pdf) (p. 10).



## Core Analytical Components
The following table outlines the main modules investigated during the spreadsheet simulation phase.

| Analysis Area | Data Scope | Applied Methodology |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Revenue Stream** | Ticket sales and city subsidies | Demand estimation relative to price (final_report.pdf) (p. 7) |
| **Cost Structure** | Fuel, maintenance, and wages | Variable and fixed cost analysis (final_report.pdf) (p. 7), (final_report.pdf) (p. 14) |
| **Sensitivity Analysis** | External factor fluctuations | Tornado and Spider charts (final_report.pdf) (p. 10), (final_report.pdf) (p. 15) |

## Strategic Decision Scenarios
The simulation evaluated two distinct policy directions. The social oriented strategy assumes the minimization of ticket prices while optimizing internal costs and promoting sustainable development(final_report.pdf) (p. 7), (final_report.pdf) (p. 12). Conversely, the profit oriented strategy focuses on increasing ticket revenue to maximize net profit for dynamic communication network expansion(final_report.pdf) (p. 7), (final_report.pdf) (p. 10).

## HTML Interactive Presentation
As an additional technical component, the project results are presented through a custom built interactive HTML interface. This presentation layer was designed to translate complex raw data from the Excel simulations into clear, visual insights. It allows users to navigate through financial sensitivity results, empirical regression equations, and final strategic recommendations in a modern web based format.



## Conclusions
The combination of rigorous Excel based simulations and an interactive HTML presentation demonstrates a modern approach to business analytics. The model successfully identifies how fleet failure rates and wage levels impact overall profitability, providing a clear roadmap for urban transport management(final_report.pdf) (p. 10), (final_report.pdf) (p. 11).
